As part of a **fluid mechanics dissection project**, my team analyzed a handheld leaf blower. The objective was to disassemble the system, identify key components, and explain how combustion-driven motion produces airflow, with an emphasis on the fluid-mechanical behavior of the system. Rather than treating the engine as an isolated power source, we focused on how it functions as an integrated engine-flywheel-impeller assembly that converts mechanical work into controlled air flow.

[View the Fluid Mechanical Dissection Video on YouTube](https://youtu.be/z-tMtfXQWRo)

Our group disassembled the housing to expose the internal components and analyzed how energy is transferred through the system from a fluid-mechanical perspective. In particular, we examined the role of the flywheel in stabilizing rotational speed and the geometry and function of the impeller responsible for accelerating air. We traced how pressure-driven piston motion leads to crankshaft rotation, which drives the flywheel and impeller, producing airflow through the blower housing. This analysis connected combustion, rotational motion, and impeller-driven flow as a continuous fluid-mechanical process.

![Inside Leafblower]({{ "/assets/images/inside-blower.jpg" | relative_url }}){: .inline-image-l}

My individual contribution focused on the final technical portion of the video, where I connected the physical dissection to fluid-mechanical principles. I developed and explained a piston–cylinder schematic to illustrate piston motion, port timing, and volume change in a two-stroke cycle. Using this diagram, I related piston motion to pressure and volume behavior during intake, compression, combustion, and exhaust, and introduced quantitative values and assumptions such as engine displacement, operating speed, and pressure behavior. I explained the purpose of each equation used, what each variable represents physically, and how these values correspond to observable engine behavior. This analysis clarified how combustion-generated work drives rotational motion that is ultimately used by the flywheel and impeller to generate airflow.
