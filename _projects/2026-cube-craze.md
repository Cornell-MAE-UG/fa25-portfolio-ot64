#### **Summary**
As part of a three-person team in my Mechatronics course, I led the mechanical design of our competition robot, conceiving the square cardboard enclosure that would trap and collect blocks as the robot drove over them. While budget and manufacturing constraints forced us to simplify from our original vision, the core design proved effective enough to win our first two matches. I also contributed significantly to the Arduino code, which used direct register manipulation on the ATmega328P to control motors and read QTI line sensors, executing a pre-programmed spiral sweep pattern across the field. The project gave me hands-on experience translating a design concept into a functional system under real constraints of time, budget, and hardware reliability.
